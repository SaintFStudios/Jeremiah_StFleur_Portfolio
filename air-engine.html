Course Project (EML2322L)

Compressed Air Engine & Powered Slicer

A complete design-manufacture-assemble-test cycle project involving a machined air engine and an engine-powered slicing mechanism.

Jan 2025 - May 2025 Mechanical Design & Academic
DFM CNC/Lathe Tolerance Analysis Mechanism Design

Project Overview

This academic project involved the complete engineering design cycle: from conceptualization and detailed design to manufacturing, assembly, testing, and documentation. The compressed air engine serves as a power source for a mechanical slicing mechanism.

Design Constraints & Specifications

  • Part Count: 4-12 machined components
  • Manufacturing: ≤1 CNC operation, primarily manual machining (lathe, mill)
  • Budget: ≤$75 total cost
  • Engine Performance: Target RPM: 500-800 under 40 PSI input
  • Slicer Mechanism: Rocker four-bar with ~3.5" stroke length

Manufacturing & Assembly

  • Engine Components: Produced 3 interchangeable builds with "thou-level" fits (±0.001-0.003" tolerance)
  • Machining Processes: Lathe turning, milling, drilling, tapping, CNC contouring
  • Material Selection: Aluminum 6061 for engine, UHMW for slider, PETG for chassis
  • Assembly: Press-fit blade holder, clearance fits for moving parts, sealed air connections

Engineering Documentation

  • DFM: Issued revised drawings with tolerance stack-up analysis
  • ECNs: Documented 5 major design revisions
  • MIRs: Quality control for all machined parts
  • BOM: Costed BOM with vendor sourcing; leveraged OTS stainless brackets to reduce machining time by 30%

Performance Results

  • Engine achieved 650 RPM at 40 PSI (meeting design target)
  • Slicer successfully cut through test materials at operating speed
  • All machined parts passed dimensional inspection within spec
  • Total project cost: $68 (under $75 budget)
